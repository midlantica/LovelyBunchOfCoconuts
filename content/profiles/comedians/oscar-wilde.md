## Oscar Wilde

Oscar Wilde was the supreme wit of the Victorian age, whose epigrams remain as sharp and funny today as when he first delivered them. His plays — The Importance of Being Earnest chief among them — are masterclasses in comic construction. Wilde proved that the best comedy is indistinguishable from the best philosophy.
