## Federalist 51: Ambition Checks Ambition

In 1788 James Madison wrote Federalist No. 51 and explained the genius of the Constitution in one line:

**"Ambition must be made to counteract ambition."**

The problem: How do you limit government power when the people running government want more power?

Madison's answer: Don't rely on virtue. Rely on structure.

**Make the branches compete against each other.**

### The system:

- Congress makes laws (but the President can veto)
- President enforces laws (but Congress controls the purse)
- Courts interpret laws (but can't enforce them)
- Senate checks the House
- States check the federal government

Each branch has its own powers and its own incentives to defend them.

The result: Gridlock by design.

Madison knew this would frustrate people. That was the point.

**"If men were angels, no government would be necessary."**

### But men aren't angels. So you need a system where:

- Power is divided
- Ambition checks ambition
- No one can accumulate enough power to tyrannize

The left hates this. They want "efficient" government that can "get things done."

Madison knew better: Efficient tyranny is still tyranny.

Separation of powers protects liberty—even when it's inconvenient.
