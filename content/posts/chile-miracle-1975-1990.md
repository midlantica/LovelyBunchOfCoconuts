## Chile Miracle 1975–1990

1973 Chile under Allende: 600% inflation, empty shops, expropriations, Marxist chaos.

After Pinochet, the "Chicago Boys" launched radical free-market reforms:

- Privatized pensions, mines, utilities
- Flat 10% income tax
- Opened trade, slashed tariffs
- Independent central bank

1975–1990: average 7% annual growth. Poverty crashed from 45% to 15% by 2000.

Even left-wing governments after 1990 kept the model.

Chile leapt from socialist ruin to Latin America's richest, most stable economy—proof the formula works anywhere.
