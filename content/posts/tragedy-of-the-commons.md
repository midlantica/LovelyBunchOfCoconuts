## Tragedy of the Commons

In 1968 Garrett Hardin published an essay that explained why socialism always fails:

**"The Tragedy of the Commons"**

His example was simple:

Imagine a pasture open to all. Every herder benefits from adding more cattle. But the cost (overgrazing) is shared by everyone.

Result: Each herder adds more cattle until the pasture is destroyed.

**When everyone owns something, no one takes care of it.**

This explains everything:

- Why public parks are trashed but private yards are pristine
- Why government housing projects decay but private homes are maintained
- Why fisheries collapse but cattle ranches thrive
- Why Soviet factories polluted but private companies innovate

The solution isn't "more government."

The solution is **private property rights.**

When someone owns something, they have every incentive to preserve it, improve it, and pass it on.

When everyone owns it, everyone exploits it until it's gone.

Hardin's insight destroys the socialist fantasy:

Collective ownership doesn't create shared prosperity.

It creates shared destruction.

The left still pushes "the commons" as an ideal.

Hardin proved it's a death sentence.
