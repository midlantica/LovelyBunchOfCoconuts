## Hayek's Fatal Conceit 1988

In 1988, at age 89, Friedrich Hayek dropped his final bombshell: _The Fatal Conceit: The Errors of Socialism_.

Socialism's core mistake is the belief that a few minds can consciously design a better order than the spontaneous order that billions create through free choices.

Civilisation is not the product of reason—it is the product of the extended order of markets, morals, language, law, and money that evolved over millennia.

This order is far wiser than any planner because it uses knowledge no single brain can hold: prices signal scarcity, traditions encode survival lessons, competition weeds out bad ideas.

To replace it with central planning is the height of arrogance—the "fatal conceit."

Hayek wrote it as Communism was visibly dying. Three years later the Soviet Union was gone.

The 20th century's greatest experiment in rational design ended in bread lines and mass graves. The unplanned order of liberty won again.
