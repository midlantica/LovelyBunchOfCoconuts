## Negative vs Positive Rights: Freedom FROM vs Entitlement TO

There are two types of "rights"—and only one is real.

**Negative Rights**: Freedom FROM interference.

**Positive Rights**: Entitlement TO something from others.

The difference matters.

**Negative Rights (Real Rights):**

- Freedom of speech (government can't silence you)
- Freedom of religion (government can't force a religion on you)
- Right to bear arms (government can't disarm you)
- Right to property (government can't steal from you)

These rights require one thing: **Leave me alone.**

They cost nothing. They don't force anyone to do anything. They simply prohibit interference.

**Positive Rights (Fake Rights):**

- "Right" to healthcare (someone must provide it)
- "Right" to housing (someone must build/pay for it)
- "Right" to education (someone must teach/fund it)
- "Right" to a job (someone must employ you)

These "rights" require: **Give me something.**

They cost money. They force others to provide. They violate real rights.

Here's the test:

**If your "right" requires forcing someone else to work or pay, it's not a right—it's theft.**

The left loves positive rights because they sound compassionate:

"Everyone deserves healthcare!"

But what they mean is: "Force doctors to work and taxpayers to pay."

That's not a right. That's slavery with extra steps.

The Founders understood this:

The Bill of Rights is all negative rights—prohibitions on government power.

They never claimed you have a "right" to force others to give you things.

Negative rights protect freedom.

Positive rights destroy it.

Choose wisely.
