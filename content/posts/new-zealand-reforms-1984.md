## New Zealand Reforms 1984-1993

By 1984 New Zealand was dying.

### Decades of socialist policies had turned a prosperous nation into a basket case:

- Massive deficits
- 15% unemployment
- Inflation at 17%
- Price controls on everything
- Import licenses for bananas

The government controlled wages, prices, interest rates, and foreign exchange.

Then Labour Finance Minister Roger Douglas did the unthinkable:

**He unleashed capitalism.**

### The reforms were brutal and fast:

- Slashed tariffs from 40% to 5%
- Eliminated subsidies
- Privatized state industries
- Deregulated everything
- Cut top tax rate from 66% to 33%
- Floated the currency

Unions screamed. Socialists rioted. The left called it "Rogernomics" as an insult.

### The results:

- Inflation crushed to 2%
- Budget balanced
- Economy grew 4% per year
- Unemployment fell
- New Zealand became competitive again

The lesson: Even a socialist government can save a nation—if it abandons socialism.

Douglas proved that free markets work even when socialists implement them.
