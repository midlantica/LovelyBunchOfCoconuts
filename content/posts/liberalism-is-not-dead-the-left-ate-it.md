## Liberalism Isn’t dead, don't confuse it with Leftism.

Liberalism is just buried under 120 years of collectivist garbage.

The core of liberalism is dead-simple:

- You own your life
- You own your body
- You own the fruits of your labor
- You get to speak, worship, assemble, and trade with whomever you want
- The government’s only job is to stop other people from violating those rights
- Equal rules for everyone, no special privileges for no one

That’s it. Everything else is commentary.

From roughly 1688 (Glorious Revolution) to 1914, the parts of the world that actually practiced this (Britain, the Netherlands, the United States, etc.) gave mankind the single greatest explosion of freedom, wealth, invention, and living standards in recorded history.

A very partial list of what freedom delivered in one century:

railroads · steamships · telegraph · telephone · electric light & power · recorded sound · photography · motion pictures · automobiles · airplanes · radio · X-rays · quantum physics · germ theory · anesthesia · antiseptics · the periodic table · skyscrapers · mass steel & aluminum · aspirin · diesel engines · indoor plumbing for the masses · real wages up 500–1000 % for ordinary workers · life expectancy from ~30 to ~70

Name another system that comes within an order of magnitude of that scorecard. I’ll wait.

Then the Marxists showed up and said “Equality before the law isn’t enough — you need enforced equality of outcome, and we’ll deliver it at gunpoint.”

Liberalism, because it actually believes in free speech and tolerance, let them talk.
**Big mistake.**

Gramsci told them to march through the institutions instead of the streets.
They did.

We laughed at the bearded weirdos in 1968.
Their students now run the universities, the NGOs, the HR departments, the media, and half the government bureaucracies.

We beat the USSR but imported the ideology anyway.

Today, the idea that the government should leave you alone is considered “far-right.”
That is how completely the Overton window has been dragged left.

In 1900, a 90 % top marginal tax rate and the state running your healthcare, education, and retirement would have been considered literal tyranny.
In 2025, suggesting we get rid of those things is considered literal tyranny.

That’s not “liberalism failed.”
That’s “we stopped being liberals and became soft-socialists while pretending we didn’t.”

And yeah, classical liberals in the 19th century were generally pro-free-migration because most countries still had small welfare states and tiny governments. Times have changed — massive vote-buying welfare states plus open borders is a suicide pact, not a liberal policy. So we adapt that part. The rest of the program is still perfect.

**Bottom line:**
I don’t defend liberalism because it created the wealthiest, healthiest, most innovative century in human history (even though it did).

I defend it because it is the **only system** that treats you like a free, adult human being instead of a toddler who needs the state to wipe your nose and plan your life.

Anyone arguing against maximum freedom is, whether they admit it or not, arguing for some flavor of imposed control.
That’s the Left, full stop.

**Freedom > everything else.**
And it just so happens the freest societies also became the richest, safest, and coolest.
Funny how that works.
