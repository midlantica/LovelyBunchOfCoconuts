## Price Controls Always Fail

Every generation, politicians rediscover price controls.

Every generation, they fail the same way.

**Price ceilings create shortages. Price floors create surpluses.**

It's not complicated:

**Rent Control** (price ceiling on housing):

- Landlords can't profit, so they stop building
- Existing buildings decay (why maintain what you can't profit from?)
- Housing shortage gets worse
- Black markets emerge

Result: San Francisco, New York—sky-high rents despite "rent control."

**Minimum Wage** (price floor on labor):

- Employers can't afford workers at the mandated price
- Low-skilled workers get fired or never hired
- Unemployment rises among the people it's supposed to help

Result: Teen unemployment, especially among minorities, skyrockets.

**Price Ceilings on Gasoline** (1970s):

- Gas stations ran out of gas
- Lines stretched for blocks
- Rationing and black markets

Result: The "energy crisis" was a government-created shortage.

Why do price controls always fail?

**Because prices communicate information.**

High prices signal: "We need more of this."

Low prices signal: "We have too much of this."

When government overrides price signals, it destroys the information system that coordinates supply and demand.

The result is always the same: Shortages, surpluses, and black markets.

Thomas Sowell said it best:

**"Price controls are the most effective way to create shortages ever devised by man."**

Politicians keep trying them anyway.
