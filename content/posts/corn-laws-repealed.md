## Corn Laws Repealed 1846

For decades the Corn Laws kept foreign grain out and bread prices high for the poor.

Cobden, Bright, and classical liberals fought. In 1846 Conservative PM Robert Peel finally repealed them.

The aristocracy predicted ruin.

### Instead:

- Bread prices fell 25–40 %
- Real wages soared
- British farms grew richer on meat and dairy

Britain fed Europe while its own people doubled and prospered.

One vote proved free trade makes everyone richer.
