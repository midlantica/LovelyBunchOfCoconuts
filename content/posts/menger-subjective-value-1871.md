## Carl Menger: Value is Subjective

In 1871 Carl Menger published _Principles of Economics_ and destroyed the labor theory of value with one insight:

**Value is subjective.**

For centuries, economists believed value came from labor:

- A coat is valuable because it took 10 hours to make
- A diamond is valuable because it's hard to mine
- Price = labor input

Menger proved this was backwards.

Value doesn't come from labor. It comes from **human preferences**.

His example:

A glass of water in the desert is worth more than a diamond.

Why? Because the thirsty person values it more.

The same glass of water by a river is worthless.

Same labor. Same object. Different value.

**Value is in the mind of the valuer, not in the object itself.**

This destroyed socialism's foundation:

If value comes from labor, workers are "exploited" when they don't get the full value.

But if value is subjective, there is no "true" value to be exploited from.

Price is simply what buyer and seller agree on—both benefit, or they wouldn't trade.

Menger launched the Austrian School of Economics and proved:

- Markets discover value through exchange
- Central planners can't calculate value
- Socialism is impossible

Marx said labor creates value.

Menger proved preferences create value.

Guess who was right?
