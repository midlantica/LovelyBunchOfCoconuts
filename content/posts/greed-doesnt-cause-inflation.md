## Greed Doesn't Cause Inflation or High Prices

Every time prices rise, politicians blame "corporate greed."

It's a lie. Here's why:

**If greed causes high prices, why weren't prices high last year?**

Were corporations not greedy in 2019? Did they suddenly become greedy in 2022?

Of course not. Corporations are always trying to maximize profit.

So why do prices change?

**Supply and demand. Not greed.**

### When prices rise, it's because:

- Supply decreased (fewer goods available)
- Demand increased (more money chasing same goods)
- Money supply increased (inflation)

### When prices fall, it's because:

- Supply increased (more goods available)
- Demand decreased (less money chasing goods)

Greed is constant. Prices change. Therefore, greed doesn't cause price changes.

**The "greed" test:**

If greed causes high prices, why don't companies charge $1,000 for a gallon of milk?

Because no one would buy it. Competition limits prices.

If one company raises prices too high, customers buy from competitors.

### The only way to maintain high prices is to:

- Eliminate competition (government-granted monopoly)
- Print money (government-caused inflation)
- Restrict supply (government regulations)

Notice the pattern? **Government** enables high prices, not greed.

### Politicians blame greed because admitting the truth means admitting _they_ caused the problem:

- Fed printed $5 trillion → inflation
- Regulations restrict supply → shortages
- Tariffs limit competition → higher prices

Blaming greed is easier than taking responsibility.

Milton Friedman said it best:

**"The world runs on individuals pursuing their self-interest. The great achievements of civilization have not come from government bureaus."**

Greed built your iPhone, your car, and your grocery store.

Government printed the money that made them expensive.
