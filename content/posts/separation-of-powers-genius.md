## Separation of Powers: Constitutional Genius

The Founders faced an impossible problem:

**How do you create a government strong enough to protect liberty but not strong enough to destroy it?**

Their solution: **Separation of Powers.**

Instead of one sovereign authority, they created three competing branches:

**Legislative Branch** (Congress):

- Makes the laws
- Controls spending
- Can override vetoes
- Can impeach the President

**Executive Branch** (President):

- Enforces the laws
- Commands military
- Can veto legislation
- Appoints judges

**Judicial Branch** (Courts):

- Interprets the laws
- Can strike down unconstitutional acts
- Protects individual rights
- Cannot enforce its own rulings

The genius: Each branch has power, but none has all the power.

They compete. They check each other. They frustrate each other.

This isn't a bug—it's the feature.

Montesquieu warned: "When legislative and executive powers are united in the same person... there can be no liberty."

The Founders listened.

The left wants to eliminate this. They push for:

- Executive orders that bypass Congress
- Court-packing to control the judiciary
- Administrative agencies that make, enforce, and judge their own rules

They call it "progress."

The Founders called it tyranny.

Separation of powers is slow, messy, and frustrating.

It's also the only thing standing between liberty and despotism.
