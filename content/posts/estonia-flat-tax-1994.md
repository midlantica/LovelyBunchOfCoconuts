## Estonia Flat Tax Revolution 1994

In 1991 Estonia broke free from the Soviet Union—bankrupt, broken, and occupied for 50 years.

Three years later, they did something radical:

**They adopted a 26% flat tax.**

One rate. No deductions. No loopholes. No complexity.

The tax code fit on one page.

Economists said it was reckless. "You need progressive taxation! You need complexity! You need to soak the rich!"

Estonia ignored them.

### The results:

- GDP growth averaged 7% per year
- Foreign investment flooded in
- Government revenue _increased_ (because people actually paid)
- Estonia became the "Baltic Tiger"

By 2005, Estonia had the fastest-growing economy in Europe.

Why did it work?

**Simple taxes = less evasion, less corruption, more growth.**

When everyone pays the same rate, there's no incentive to cheat, no army of lobbyists writing loopholes, and no politicians picking winners.

Today, over 40 countries have adopted flat taxes.

Estonia proved that simplicity beats complexity, and freedom beats central planning.

Even after 50 years of Soviet destruction.
