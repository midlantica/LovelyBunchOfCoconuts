## Bastiat's Government Paradox

Bastiat's devastating one-liner (1850):

"The state is that great fiction by which everyone tries to live at the expense of everyone else."

And the perfect follow-up:

People say: "We don't want the government to grow grain."

Very well.

But then they add: "We want the government to feed the people, create jobs, give free healthcare, subsidise art, guarantee loans…"

It is as if they said:

"We do not want the government to grow grain—yet we demand that it magically provide bread for all."

Government has no resources of its own. It can only take from Peter to give to Paul.

Every "service" is paid for by someone who never consented.

Bastiat exposed the illusion in a single paragraph that still stings 175 years later.
