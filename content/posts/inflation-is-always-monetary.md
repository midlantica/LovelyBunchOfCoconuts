## Inflation is Always Monetary

Milton Friedman said it clearly:

**"Inflation is always and everywhere a monetary phenomenon."**

Translation: Inflation is caused by printing too much money. Period.

Not greed. Not supply chains. Not Putin. Not corporations.

**Too much money chasing too few goods.**

Here's the proof:

**1970s**: Fed printed money. Inflation hit 13%.

**1980s**: Volcker stopped printing. Inflation dropped to 3%.

**2020-2022**: Fed printed $5 trillion. Inflation hit 9%.

Every. Single. Time.

Politicians blame everyone except the people printing money:

- "Corporate greed!" (Were corporations not greedy in 2019?)
- "Supply chain disruptions!" (Why did prices rise for everything, not just disrupted goods?)
- "Putin's price hike!" (Inflation started before Ukraine)

The real cause: Central banks printing money to fund government spending.

Why do they lie?

Because admitting the truth means admitting _government_ causes inflation.

Friedman's insight destroys the excuses:

- You can't fix inflation with price controls
- You can't fix it by taxing the rich
- You can't fix it by blaming corporations

You fix it by **stopping the printing press**.

The left wants you to believe inflation is mysterious and complex.

It's not. It's simple:

Print more money = higher prices.

Stop printing = inflation stops.

Friedman proved it 50 years ago. It's still true today.
