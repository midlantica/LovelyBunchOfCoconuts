## Glorious Revolution 1688–89

In 1688 England stood on the edge of absolutist monarchy. James II was building a standing army, suspending laws by royal decree, and trying to re-impose Catholicism by force.

A group of bishops and nobles invited the Dutch prince William of Orange to invade and rescue English liberty.

William landed with a fleet larger than the Spanish Armada—yet almost no blood was shed. James II fled. Parliament passed the **English Bill of Rights (1689)**:

- No taxation without consent
- Free speech in Parliament
- No standing army in peacetime without Parliament's approval
- Right to petition the king
- Right of Protestants to bear arms

For the first time, a great nation declared that government exists to protect pre-existing rights. The state became a "night watchman." Classical Liberalism was born.
