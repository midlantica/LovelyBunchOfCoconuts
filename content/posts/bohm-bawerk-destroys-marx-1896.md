## Böhm-Bawerk Destroys Marx's Surplus Value

In 1896 Eugen von Böhm-Bawerk published _Karl Marx and the Close of His System_ and demolished the foundation of Marxist economics:

**The surplus value theory is nonsense.**

### Marx's theory claimed:

- Workers create all value through labor
- Capitalists pay workers less than the value they create
- The difference (surplus value) is theft
- Therefore: capitalism = exploitation

Böhm-Bawerk showed three fatal flaws:

**Flaw 1: The Transformation Problem**

Marx couldn't explain how labor values convert to market prices. His math didn't work. He tried to fix it in Volume 3 of _Das Kapital_—and failed.

**Flaw 2: Time Preference**

Workers want wages _now_. Capitalists wait for products to sell _later_. The "surplus" is payment for waiting and risk—not theft.

A worker who wants $50 today instead of $55 next year isn't exploited. He's making a choice.

**Flaw 3: Capital Contributes Value**

Machines, tools, and organization increase productivity. A worker with a bulldozer moves more dirt than one with a shovel.

The capitalist who provides the bulldozer adds value. The return on capital isn't theft—it's payment for contribution.

Böhm-Bawerk's conclusion:

**Marx's exploitation theory is mathematically impossible and economically illiterate.**

Socialists ignored him and kept preaching Marx.

A century later, they still can't refute Böhm-Bawerk.

They just pretend he doesn't exist.
