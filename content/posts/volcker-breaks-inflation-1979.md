## Volcker Breaks Inflation 1979-1982

By 1979 America was in crisis.

Inflation hit 13%. The dollar was collapsing. Gas lines stretched for blocks. The economy was dying.

Politicians blamed "greed" and "speculators."

Paul Volcker knew better.

In August 1979, President Carter appointed him Fed Chairman. Volcker had one mission:

**Kill inflation. No matter the cost.**

His method was brutal:

Raise interest rates until inflation breaks.

The prime rate hit 21.5%.

Mortgages became unaffordable. Businesses couldn't borrow. Unemployment spiked to 10%.

Farmers drove tractors to the Fed in protest. Homebuilders mailed him 2x4s. Politicians screamed for his head.

Volcker didn't flinch.

He knew the truth: Short-term pain beats long-term destruction.

By 1983, inflation was crushed to 3%.

The economy roared back. Reagan's boom began.

### Volcker proved three things:

1. Inflation is always a monetary phenomenon
2. Central banks cause it, and only central banks can stop it
3. Political courage beats political popularity

The left still blames "corporate greed" for inflation.

Volcker proved them wrong 40 years ago.
