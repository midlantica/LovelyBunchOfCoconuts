## Federalist 10: Factions and the Republic

In 1787 James Madison wrote Federalist No. 10 and solved a problem that had plagued humanity for millennia:

**How do you stop factions from destroying freedom?**

A faction is any group united by passion or interest adverse to the rights of others.

### History showed that factions always win:

- In pure democracies, the majority faction tyrannizes the minority
- In small republics, demagogues seize power
- In monarchies, the king's faction oppresses everyone

Madison's solution was brilliant:

**Don't eliminate factions. Control their effects.**

### How? Through a large republic with:

1. **Representation** (filters passion through elected officials)
2. **Extended territory** (makes it harder for one faction to dominate)
3. **Diverse interests** (prevents any single faction from forming a majority)

The genius: In a large, diverse republic, factions compete and check each other.

No single group can tyrannize the rest.

This is why America isn't a pure democracy—it's a constitutional republic.

Madison knew that democracy = mob rule.

A republic = rule of law that protects individual rights.

The left pushes "democracy" as the highest good.

Madison knew better: Unchecked democracy destroys liberty.

The Constitution protects us from it.
