## Great Depression: Government Prolonged It

The standard story: The Great Depression proved capitalism failed, and FDR's New Deal saved America.

The truth: Government intervention turned a recession into a decade-long depression.

**What actually happened:**

**1929**: Stock market crashes. Recession begins (normal business cycle).

**1930**: Hoover raises tariffs (Smoot-Hawley). Global trade collapses.

**1930-32**: Fed lets money supply contract 30%. Banks fail.

**1933**: FDR takes over, promises relief.

Then FDR makes it worse:

- **National Industrial Recovery Act**: Forces businesses to raise prices and wages (destroying jobs)
- **Agricultural Adjustment Act**: Pays farmers to destroy crops (while people starve)
- **Social Security**: New payroll tax during depression
- **Constant policy changes**: Businesses can't plan, so they don't invest

Result: Unemployment stays above 15% for the entire 1930s.

Compare to the 1920-21 depression:

- Deeper initial crash than 1929
- Government did nothing
- Economy recovered in 18 months

Or the 1987 crash:

- Bigger one-day drop than 1929
- Government didn't intervene
- No depression followed

The lesson: **Recessions are normal. Government intervention makes them worse.**

FDR's Treasury Secretary Henry Morgenthau admitted in 1939:

**"We have tried spending money. We are spending more than we have ever spent before and it does not work... After eight years of this administration we have just as much unemployment as when we started."**

What ended the Depression? World War II production—not New Deal programs.

The left still worships FDR.

The data says he prolonged the suffering.
