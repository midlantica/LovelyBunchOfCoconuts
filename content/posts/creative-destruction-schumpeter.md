## Creative Destruction: Progress Requires Change

In 1942 Joseph Schumpeter explained why "saving jobs" kills prosperity:

**"Creative destruction is the essential fact about capitalism."**

Here's how it works:

New innovations destroy old industries—and that's _good_.

### Examples:

- Cars destroyed the horse-and-buggy industry
- Electricity destroyed the candle industry
- Computers destroyed the typewriter industry
- Netflix destroyed Blockbuster

Each time, people screamed: "What about the jobs?!"

Schumpeter's answer: Those workers move to _better_ jobs in the new industries.

The buggy-whip makers became auto workers. The typewriter repairmen became IT specialists.

**Progress requires destruction of the old to make room for the new.**

### When government tries to "save jobs" by protecting dying industries:

- Resources stay trapped in obsolete sectors
- Innovation slows
- Everyone stays poorer

### Examples of failed "job saving":

- Steel tariffs (saved a few steel jobs, killed thousands of manufacturing jobs)
- Auto bailouts (rewarded failure, punished success)
- Coal subsidies (kept workers in dying industry instead of retraining)

The left wants to freeze the economy in place to "protect workers."

Schumpeter proved this guarantees stagnation.

Creative destruction is painful in the short term.

But it's the only path to long-term prosperity.

You can have progress or you can have protected jobs.

You can't have both.
