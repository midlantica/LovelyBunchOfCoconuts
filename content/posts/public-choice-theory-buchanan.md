## Public Choice Theory: Politicians Aren't Angels

In 1962 James Buchanan and Gordon Tullock published _The Calculus of Consent_ and destroyed a myth that had ruled for centuries:

**Politicians are just as self-interested as everyone else.**

Before Public Choice Theory, economists assumed:

- Markets fail because people are greedy
- Government fixes markets because politicians are noble
- Bureaucrats serve "the public interest"

Buchanan asked one question:

**Why would self-interest disappear when someone enters government?**

It doesn't.

Politicians want re-election. Bureaucrats want bigger budgets. Interest groups want subsidies.

The result: Government "solutions" that benefit politicians and special interests—not the public.

Examples everywhere:

- Farm subsidies (enriching agribusiness, not feeding the poor)
- Occupational licensing (protecting incumbents, not consumers)
- Tariffs (helping connected industries, hurting everyone else)

Public Choice Theory explains why government fails:

**Not because politicians are evil, but because incentives are broken.**

Buchanan won the Nobel Prize in 1986.

His insight remains devastating: You can't fix market failure with government failure.

The left still pretends politicians are angels.

Buchanan proved they're just people—with power.
