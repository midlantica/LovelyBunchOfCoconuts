## 1920-21 Depression: Government Did Nothing, Economy Recovered

In 1920 America faced a depression worse than 1929:

- Stock market crashed 47%
- Unemployment hit 12%
- GDP fell 17%
- Wholesale prices dropped 40%

It was brutal. Banks failed. Businesses closed. Workers lost jobs.

Then something remarkable happened:

**The government did nothing.**

No stimulus. No bailouts. No price controls. No New Deal.

President Harding's approach: Cut spending, cut taxes, let the market adjust.

The result:

**By 1922, the depression was over.**

- Unemployment back to 3%
- Production soaring
- The Roaring Twenties began

Total recovery time: 18 months.

Compare to the Great Depression:

- Government intervened massively
- Unemployment stayed above 15% for a decade
- Recovery took 15+ years

Why did doing nothing work?

**Because markets adjust when you let them.**

In 1920-21:

- Prices fell (making goods affordable)
- Wages adjusted (making hiring profitable)
- Bad businesses failed (freeing resources for good ones)
- Capital flowed to productive uses

No one remembers the 1920-21 depression because it ended so fast.

Everyone remembers the Great Depression because government intervention prolonged it.

The lesson is clear:

**The best thing government can do during a recession is get out of the way.**

Keynesians ignore this example.

It destroys their entire worldview.
