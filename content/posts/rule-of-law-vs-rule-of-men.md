## Rule of Law vs Rule of Men

Civilization depends on one principle:

**Rule of Law, not Rule of Men.**

The difference:

**Rule of Law**: Fixed, known rules apply equally to everyone—including rulers.

**Rule of Men**: Rulers decide case-by-case based on their whims, preferences, or politics.

Why does this matter?

### \*Under Rule of Law:

- You know the rules in advance
- You can plan your life
- Property is secure
- Contracts are enforced
- Investment flows
- Prosperity follows

### Under Rule of Men:

- Rules change arbitrarily
- You can't plan
- Property can be seized
- Contracts are worthless
- Capital flees
- Poverty follows

History proves this:

**England after Magna Carta (1215)**: Rule of law → prosperity.

**France under Louis XIV**: Rule of men → revolution.

**Hong Kong under British law**: Rule of law → richest city in Asia.

**Hong Kong under Chinese rule**: Rule of men → capital flight.

The left hates rule of law because it limits their power.

### They prefer:

- "Prosecutorial discretion" (selective enforcement)
- "Living Constitution" (judges make it up as they go)
- "Equity" over equality (different rules for different groups)
- Executive orders (bypassing Congress)

These are all forms of rule of men.

When rulers can change the rules at will, no one is safe.

When the law is fixed and known, everyone can prosper.

The Founders knew this:

**"A government of laws, not of men."** — John Adams

Rule of law protects the weak from the powerful.

Rule of men protects the powerful from accountability.

Choose wisely.
