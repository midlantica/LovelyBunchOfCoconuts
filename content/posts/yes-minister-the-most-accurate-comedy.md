## Yes Minister: The Most Accurate Comedy Ever Made

Most comedies ask you to suspend disbelief. *Yes Minister* asks you to do the opposite: to accept, with mounting horror, that what you are watching is entirely, precisely, and uncomfortably true.

Margaret Thatcher — not a woman known for her sense of humour — reportedly said that *Yes Minister* was her favourite television programme, because it was so accurate. This is either the greatest compliment ever paid to a comedy, or the most alarming thing a Prime Minister has ever said. Possibly both.

The show, which ran from 1980 to 1988 across two series (*Yes Minister* and *Yes, Prime Minister*), was written by Antony Jay and Jonathan Lynn, and it had a simple, devastating premise: politicians believe they run the country. Civil servants actually do. The politician — Jim Hacker, Minister for Administrative Affairs, later Prime Minister — arrives in office full of ideas and intentions. His Permanent Secretary, Sir Humphrey Appleby, ensures that none of these ideas are ever implemented, using a combination of procedural obstruction, bureaucratic inertia, and language of such magnificent opacity that Hacker is never entirely sure what has just happened to him.

Sir Humphrey's ability to say nothing in two hundred words while appearing to say everything is a masterclass in the art of obfuscation. His sentences are architectural achievements — vast, load-bearing structures of subordinate clauses that collapse, upon examination, into perfect emptiness. He is never rude. He is never dishonest. He simply ensures that the truth is never quite reached.

His most famous line — delivered whenever a minister proposes something that might actually change anything — is: "That would be a very courageous decision, Minister." In Sir Humphrey's vocabulary, *courageous* means *catastrophic*. The minister always understands, eventually.

The show's central joke has never been refuted. If anything, it has been confirmed, repeatedly, by everyone who has ever worked in government and subsequently written a memoir.

*Yes Minister* is not a comedy. It is a documentary. The laugh track is the only fictional element.
