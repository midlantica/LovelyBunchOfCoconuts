## Market Failure is Rarer Than Government Failure

The left loves to claim "market failure" justifies government intervention.

But they ignore a bigger problem: **Government failure.**

**What is market failure?**

When markets don't produce the "optimal" outcome according to economic theory.

Examples leftists cite:

- Monopolies
- Externalities (pollution)
- Public goods (lighthouses, roads)
- Information asymmetry

**What is government failure?**

When government intervention makes things worse than the market "failure" it tried to fix.

Examples everywhere:

- **Monopolies**: Usually created by government (patents, licenses, regulations that block competition)
- **Pollution**: Worst under government ownership (Soviet Union destroyed the environment)
- **Public goods**: Private lighthouses existed for centuries; toll roads work fine
- **Information asymmetry**: Government regulations often hide information or create false confidence

The key insight:

**Markets fail sometimes. Government fails almost always.**

Why?

**Markets have feedback:**

- Bad businesses lose money and fail
- Customers switch to competitors
- Entrepreneurs innovate around problems

**Government has no feedback:**

- Bad programs get more funding
- No competition to switch to
- Innovation is illegal without permission

Milton Friedman's test:

**"If you put the federal government in charge of the Sahara Desert, in five years there'd be a shortage of sand."**

The left's logic:

1. Market isn't perfect
2. Therefore, government should intervene
3. Ignore that government is even less perfect

The reality:

**Imperfect markets beat perfect government plans every time.**

Because markets adjust. Government doesn't.

Markets learn from failure. Government doubles down.

Markets innovate. Government regulates.

The question isn't "Is the market perfect?"

The question is "Compared to what?"

And compared to government, markets win every time.
