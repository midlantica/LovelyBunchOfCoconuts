## Adam Smith's Self-Interest Breakthrough

In 1776 Adam Smith published _The Wealth of Nations_ and destroyed centuries of economic confusion with one sentence:

**"It is not from the benevolence of the butcher, the brewer, or the baker that we expect our dinner, but from their regard to their own interest."**

This was revolutionary.

### Before Smith, people thought:

- Trade is zero-sum (one side wins, one loses)
- Merchants are greedy parasites
- Only government planning can create prosperity

Smith showed the opposite:

**Self-interest + free exchange = mutual benefit.**

The butcher doesn't feed you out of charity. He feeds you because _he wants your money_. You give him money because _you want his meat_. Both win.

No king commanded it. No bureaucrat planned it. No one needed to be "good."

The invisible hand of the market turned private self-interest into public prosperity—automatically.

This insight launched modern economics and justified free markets forever.

Socialists still can't refute it. They just pretend self-interest disappears when you give people government power.

It doesn't.
