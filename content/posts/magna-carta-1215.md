## Magna Carta 1215

In 1215, English barons forced tyrannical King John to sign the Great Charter at Runnymede.

### Key clauses:

- No taxation without consent
- No imprisonment without lawful judgment
- Justice cannot be sold or delayed

Originally for nobles, it became the seed of liberty: even the king is under the law.

Parliament cited it for centuries to expand habeas corpus, trial by jury, and limits on royal power. Classical liberals from Coke to Blackstone called it England's ancient constitution.

The first written curb on absolute power in European history—still law in 2025.
