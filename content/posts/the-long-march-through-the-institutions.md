## The Long March Through the Institutions: A Slow-Motion Cultural Coup

After World War II, many European Marxist intellectuals realized that the old dream of a violent proletarian revolution (storming the Winter Palace, guillotine-the-bourgeoisie style) was dead. The working class in the West had been bought off with refrigerators, cars, and Sunday barbecues. Armed uprising wasn’t going to happen.

Enter the German student radical **Rudi Dutschke** in 1967. Borrowing directly from the Italian communist **Antonio Gramsci’s** theories of cultural hegemony, Dutschke popularized the phrase **“the long march through the institutions”**. The plan was simple but diabolical:

> Instead of trying to seize power by force (which would get you crushed by the state), you infiltrate and capture the institutions that shape culture and consciousness: universities, schools, media, publishing houses, churches, the civil service, HR departments, museums, the arts, and eventually even corporate boardrooms.

You do it patiently, generation after generation, by becoming the professors, the journalists, the teachers, the scriptwriters, the diversity officers, the curators. You change the language, rewrite the history books, shift what is considered “normal,” and make your once-radical ideas the new compulsory orthodoxy. Anyone who objects is labeled a bigot, a reactionary, or—today’s favorite—a “fascist.”

The **Frankfurt School** (Marcuse, Adorno, Horkheimer, Habermas, et al.) had already laid the intellectual groundwork in the 1930s–50s, blending Marx with Freud and a deep hatred of Western civilization itself. When they fled Nazi Germany and landed in American universities (especially Columbia), they began the infection in earnest. By the late 60s their students—now tenured—were running entire departments.

## What followed was a fifty-year conquest:

- **1970s**: “Political correctness” starts as an in-joke on the left, then becomes mandatory speech.
- **1980s**: Critical theory metastasizes into Women’s Studies, Black Studies, Queer Studies—entire academic fields built not to pursue truth but to advance ideological warfare.
- **1990s**: The media class graduates from the same universities and brings the new dogma into newsrooms and Hollywood.
- **2000s–2010s**: HR departments and “diversity & inclusion” become the corporate Stasi, enforcing the new secular religion.
- **2020s**: Even kindergarten curricula, library story hours, and scientific bodies are captured. Dissent is not just wrong—it is morally illegitimate, worthy of cancellation, firing, deplatforming.

The result? A civilization that no longer believes in itself, that teaches its children to be ashamed of their history, that polices every word and thought, that replaced individual merit with bureaucratic ideology, and that now struggles to even define “woman” without a 400-page DEI manual.

**The Long March worked.** The revolutionaries never needed rifles. They just needed tenure, microphones, and a lot of patience.

And the scariest part: most people still don’t realize the institutions have already been marched through. They think it’s just “progress.”

**Wake Up NPC, indeed.**
