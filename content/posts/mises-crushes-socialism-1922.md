## Mises Crushes Socialism 1922

In 1922, when almost every intellectual believed scientific socialism was the future, Ludwig von Mises published _Socialism_ and delivered the knockout blow.

His argument was devastatingly simple:

Without private property in the means of production, there are no real market prices.

Without real prices, there is no way to calculate what anything costs or is worth.

Without economic calculation, rational production becomes impossible.

Socialism isn't evil—it is literally impossible. Central planners cannot do the math that millions of free people do automatically every day through buying and selling.

The Soviet Union spent seventy years proving Mises right: endless shortages, misallocated factories, tractors built with no wheels, mountains of unsold goods nobody wanted.

Mises fired the shot in 1922. By 1991 the entire socialist world was bankrupt and gone. The calculation argument won.
