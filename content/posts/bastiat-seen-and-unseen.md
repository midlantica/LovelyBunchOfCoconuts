## Bastiat's Seen and Unseen

In 1850 Frédéric Bastiat wrote the clearest parable in all economics:

A shopkeeper's son breaks a window.

The crowd says: "At least the glazier gets work. What's bad for the shopkeeper is good for trade!"

Bastiat replies:

That is what is **seen**.

What is **not seen** is the new suit the shopkeeper would have bought instead. The tailor loses work. Net result: society is poorer by one window and one suit.

Every government "stimulus," every regulation, every tax creates visible winners and invisible losers.

Politicians always point to the seen (jobs "created"). Classical liberals ask about the unseen (jobs destroyed, growth prevented).

Bastiat's one short essay destroys 99% of bad economic thinking — then and now.
